FAQ Common questions
Can foreigners really buy property in Japan?
Yes. Japan places no restrictions on foreign property ownership. You can own both land and buildings with the same rights as Japanese citizens. You do not need a visa or residency. Purchasing remotely with a licensed bilingual agent is possible and common.
What is an akiya bank?
An akiya bank (空き家バンク) is an official municipal database where local governments list vacant properties available for sale or free transfer. There are over 1,000 such databases across Japan's 1,745 municipalities — all in Japanese and scattered across separate websites. AkiyaFind aggregates them into one searchable English platform.
Are there really free houses in Japan?
Yes. Some municipalities list akiya at ¥0 (zero yen) to encourage repopulation of rural areas. Many also offer renovation grants worth ¥500,000–¥5,000,000. The total subsidy package can exceed the property's price. We flag these listings clearly.
What do the subscription plans include?
The free Explorer plan lets you browse listings with basic details. The Searcher plan ($4/mo) unlocks full property details, agent contacts, email alerts, and saved searches. The Buyer plan ($12/mo) adds hazard map overlays, renovation subsidy lookups, priority alerts, and email concierge support.
How often are listings updated?
Our crawler refreshes listings from municipal and commercial sources every 24 hours. Paid Buyer plan members receive real-time alerts within one hour of a new match being published.
